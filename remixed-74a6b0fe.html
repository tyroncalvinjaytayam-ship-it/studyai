Category 01
Studying & Research
N

NotebookLM

by Google · Free

Upload your notes, textbooks, or lecture slides and have a real conversation with them. It only answers based on what you give it — no hallucinations.

Best for: exam prep
How to use it
1
Go to notebooklm.google.com and sign in with Google
2
Click "New Notebook" and upload your PDF notes, slides, or paste text
3
In the chat, ask it to explain a concept, make a summary, or create practice questions
4
Use the "Audio Overview" feature to get a podcast-style summary you can listen to
P

Perplexity AI

Free plan available

Like a smarter Google. It searches the web and gives you a clean, cited answer — perfect when you need real sources for a research paper.

Best for: finding sources
How to use it
1
Go to perplexity.ai — no account needed to start
2
Type your research question like you'd ask a teacher — in full sentences
3
Look at the numbered citations on the right — click them to verify and use as references
4
Use "Focus: Academic" mode to search only scholarly sources
C

Claude AI

by Anthropic · Free

The best AI for understanding hard concepts. Give it a confusing paragraph from your textbook and it will explain it in plain, simple language.

Best for: understanding topics
How to use it
1
Go to claude.ai and create a free account
2
Paste the confusing text and say: "Explain this like I'm 16 years old"
3
Ask it to create a study guide from your notes or give you practice quiz questions
4
Tell it "Quiz me" and answer its questions to test yourself before the exam
E

Elicit

Free plan available

Finds and summarizes academic research papers automatically. Saves hours when doing a literature review or thesis research.

Best for: academic papers
How to use it
1
Go to elicit.com and sign up for free
2
Type your research question — e.g. "Does social media affect student mental health?"
3
It shows real academic papers with auto-generated summaries — read the summaries first
4
Export the papers and citations directly to use in your bibliography
Category 02
Essays & Writing
G

ChatGPT

by OpenAI · Free

The most powerful essay assistant. Use it to brainstorm ideas, create outlines, draft paragraphs, and strengthen your arguments — not write it for you.

Best for: essay structure
How to use it
1
Go to chatgpt.com and sign up free
2
Tell it: "Help me create an outline for a 1000-word essay about [your topic]"
3
Write your draft yourself, then paste it and ask: "Make my arguments stronger"
4
Ask it to "Check if my thesis is clear" and suggest improvements
W

Wordtune

Free plan available

Rewrites your sentences to sound more natural, academic, or clear. A lifesaver for non-native English writers who want to sound more confident.

Best for: improving writing
How to use it
1
Go to wordtune.com or install the Chrome extension
2
Paste or type a sentence you're not happy with
3
Choose "Formal" tone for essays and pick the rewrite you like best
4
Use the Spices feature to add examples, statistics, or counterarguments automatically
G

Grammarly

Free plan available

Catches grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors in real time. The free version is good enough for most students and works inside Google Docs.

Best for: proofreading
How to use it
1
Go to grammarly.com and install the free Chrome extension
2
It automatically highlights errors as you type — in Google Docs, Gmail, and more
3
Click the green button to see your overall writing score and suggestions
4
Always read suggestions before accepting — sometimes they change your meaning
Q

Quillbot

Free plan available

Paraphrase text, summarize long articles, and check for plagiarism. Useful when you need to reword a source without copying it directly.

Best for: paraphrasing
How to use it
1
Go to quillbot.com — no account needed for basic use
2
Paste text from a source you want to reference and click "Paraphrase"
3
Choose "Formal" mode for academic writing — review the output before using it
4
Use the Summarizer tool to condense long articles into 3–5 key points
Category 03
Time & Task Management
N

Notion AI

Freemium · widely used

Your second brain. Organize notes, assignments, and deadlines in one place — then use AI to summarize your notes instantly before exams.

Best for: organizing everything
How to use it
1
Go to notion.so and sign up free with your student email
2
Create one page per subject — paste your notes, readings, and assignment details
3
Press Space to activate AI — ask it to summarize, translate, or simplify your notes
4
Use the free Student Template from Notion's gallery to set up fast
M

Motion

Paid · 7-day free trial

Automatically schedules your tasks into your calendar based on deadlines and priorities. You never have to manually plan your week again.

Best for: managing deadlines
How to use it
1
Go to usemotion.com and start the free trial
2
Add all your upcoming assignments and deadlines with estimated time needed
3
Motion will auto-schedule everything into your calendar — just follow the plan
4
If something urgent comes up, reschedule in one click and it reorganizes everything

The Full Student AI Workflow

From assignment to final submission — using AI at every step

1

Understand the assignment

Paste your assignment brief into Claude and ask: "What exactly is this asking me to do? Break it into steps."

Claude AI
2

Research your topic

Use Perplexity to find credible sources fast, and Elicit for academic papers. Collect 5–8 good sources.

Perplexity + Elicit
3

Take notes with AI help

Upload your sources to NotebookLM. Ask it to summarize the key arguments from each source.

NotebookLM
4

Build your essay outline

Give ChatGPT your notes and ask it to create a detailed outline — intro, 3 main points, conclusion.

ChatGPT
5

Write it yourself (important!)

Use the outline and write the essay in your own words. AI is your assistant — not your ghostwriter. This is where you actually learn.

6

30+ Copy-Paste Prompts

For studying & understanding

Explain [concept] like I'm a high school student. Use a simple real-life example.
I have an exam on [topic] tomorrow. Create a 1-page study guide with the most important points.
Quiz me on [topic]. Ask me 10 questions one at a time and tell me if I'm right or wrong.
Summarize this chapter into 5 bullet points I can review in 2 minutes: [paste chapter]
What are the most likely exam questions for a student studying [topic]?
Compare and contrast [concept A] vs [concept B] in a simple table.

For writing essays

Help me create a detailed outline for a [word count] essay about [topic]. My main argument is [your thesis].
Review my essay introduction and tell me if my thesis is clear and arguable: [paste intro]
My essay argument feels weak. Suggest 3 stronger points I could make about [topic].
Rewrite this paragraph to sound more academic and formal, without changing the meaning: [paste paragraph]
What counterarguments might someone make against my essay thesis? How should I address them?
Write a strong concluding paragraph that summarizes these main points: [list your points]

For research

What are the most important academic sources or scholars I should look up for research on [topic]?
Summarize the key arguments in this article and list 3 quotes I could use in my paper: [paste article]
I found this statistic: [stat]. Is it commonly cited? What context do I need to know before using it?
Help me write a citation for this source in [APA/MLA/Chicago] format: [paste source details]

For staying organized

I have these 5 assignments due this week: [list them with deadlines]. Help me create a daily study schedule.
I only have 2 hours to study before this exam. What should I prioritize based on these notes: [paste notes]
I'm feeling overwhelmed by [subject]. Break down exactly what I need to do to get a passing grade step by step.
